OUR HERITAGE

Over a century of uncompromising excellence, where every tick tells a story of precision, innovation, and timeless craftsmanship.

MILESTONES IN TIME

1905

The Foundation

Founded by visionary craftsman Hans Wilsdorf, our journey began with a simple yet ambitious goal: to create the most precise timepieces in the world.

1926

Waterproof Innovation

Introduction of the world's first waterproof wristwatch case, the Oyster, revolutionizing watchmaking and setting new standards for durability.

1931

Perpetual Movement

Patent of the perpetual, self-winding rotor mechanism, eliminating the need for manual winding and ensuring continuous precision.

1953

Conquering Everest

Our timepieces accompany Sir Edmund Hillary and Tenzing Norgay to the summit of Mount Everest, proving their reliability in extreme conditions.

1963

Racing Heritage

Launch of the Daytona chronograph, specifically designed for professional racing drivers and becoming an icon of motorsport precision.

1971

Deep Sea Exploration

Development of the Sea-Dweller, capable of withstanding depths of over 1,200 meters, pushing the boundaries of underwater exploration.

2000

Parachrom Innovation

Introduction of the revolutionary Parachrom hairspring, offering superior resistance to shocks and magnetic fields.

2024

Future Vision

Continuing our legacy with cutting-edge innovations while maintaining the traditional craftsmanship that defines our heritage.
